About me

Richard Xu

Developer | Student | Entrepreneur

I've seen a lot and done a lot, but I feel like there's so much more to do.
Here's just a little about me:

  • 1995

    Game Start

    I was born in the quiet town of Walnut Creek, California. My parents had moved to the states a couple years ago, and were eager to start a family in the technological bubble called Silicon Valley. My dad was a material Scientist and my mom a registered nurse, and this was the start of my adventure!

  • Sometime in 1998

    Portlandia

    Three years after I was born, my dad got relocated (thank you Fujitsu) and we moved to Beaverton, a small suburb of Portland, Oregon. In the state that was 99% forest, I grew to love the outdoors. I started discovering my interests as wide as they were, from soccer and swimming to playing with robots and gaming.

  • August 23,2004

    The day I became a cowboy

    But eventually, we had to move again, this time to Austin, Texas. I moved in 5th grade and I was that new kid in class. Luckily, southern hospitality and tons of other Asian kids in my class gave me the opportunity to make many friends that I still talk to today, until...

  • August 23, 2011

    Sleepless in Seattle

    I moved again. And this time, it sucked. I moved away from all my friends into the rainy, gloomy Bellevue, WA, near Seattle. But in retrospect, it wasn't that bad, I made new friends, and grew in my interest in chess, knowledge bowl, model United Nations, philosophy and robotics. Yeah, I think I've come to adore Seattle weather.

  • August 23, 2013

    Building, Berkeley and the Bay Area

    But nothing beats California sunshine, and that's where I am now, in sunny, liberal, beautiful Berkeley, California. I love being close to the bay and being back in Silicon Valley! And I look forward to my next few years here.

  • June 1, 2014

    UCWhereNow?

    Over the summer of 2014, I worked at UCSF as an intern in the global Health division. I really got a sense of global health disparities in this position and it really drove me to focus my career and research on leveling the playing field for everyone no matter race, gender or location.

  • June 15, 2015

    Working Hard

    Over this past summer (2015), I worked at Bio-Rad working on an internal tool to provide dynamic analysis on Digital Droplet PCR information. I honestly didn't find the project too exciting or novel, but the experience of working in an established company on an active project was well worth the 2 hour commute and sweltering heat.

Projects

Here are some of my projects:

Bio-Rad

Engineering Intern

I interned at Bio-Rad over the summer of 2015 on an internal tool to take input from the digital droplet PCR devices and store it on an internal server for instant access and dynamic analysis by the scientists. I built the tool using django, mongodb, bokeh (for plotting large data sets) and some personalized design.

UCSF

Global Health Intern

I interned at UCSF's global health division over the summer of 2014 working on redesigning the curriculum on their online platform to allow seniors to complete their capstone projects more efficiently and record their progress while abroad.

Spatial Scene Generator

Language and Cognition Laboratory
University of California, Berkeley

I perform the data analysis in the Language and Cognition Laboratory at UC Berkeley. My current project is to take the current set of TRPS (Topological Relation Picture Series) and expand the space by breaking down each scene into a set of features and comparing features across all scenes.

Indigo

LA Hacks

I collaborated with a group of 4 engineers to create Indigo, an app which uses bleeding-edge IBM cognitive technology and proprietary NLP implementations to automatically create personalized study material from students' course notes, lecture slides, and textbooks. Indigo was chosen as the runner-up winner of the IBM Bluemix API Challenge at LA Hacks.

LeapMe

Cal Hacks

I created an app to use the Leap Motion (hardware that tracks hand-motions) to perform background tasks, such as changing music or raising or lowering volume/brightness. I mostly just experimented with the hardware, since it was my first time using hardware, but it was a good learning experience.

Xendit

BitBay Hackathon

Created the winning App in the Bitbay Hackathon, that transferred money across international boarders through the use of bitcoins. Our app took in fiat currency through local vendors and transferred it within 10 second to the destined country for instant conversion to local currency.

Knox Medical Diagnostics

Design Intern

I am working as a design intern for Knox medical diagnostics, which is a startup in the bay area working towards improving lung health of children with asthma. I have learned so much about mobile and hardware design and going through the process has giving me so much insight into the design process on a product that I truly think will be a positive impact on the lives of children.
